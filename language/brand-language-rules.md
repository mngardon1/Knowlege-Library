# Brand Language & Language Rules

## Purpose
This document defines the enforced language constraints for the Knowledge Library.
These rules govern how concepts are named, introduced, and used to prevent drift,
misinterpretation, and genericization.

These are rules, not suggestions.

---

## Core Language Principles
1. Psychology precedes tactics.
2. Direction precedes execution.
3. Resonance precedes differentiation.
4. Identity precedes behavior.
5. Clarity precedes scale.

Language must always reflect these principles.

---

## Canonical Terms
The following terms are fixed and must be used consistently.

- Autocorrected Existence
- Time Sovereignty
- Full Stack Human
- Future Self Operating System
- Advantage Mapping
- Minimum Effective Offer
- Audience Identity
- Uncopyable Positioning
- Category Design
- The Self-Directed Journey Map

No alternate spellings. No synonyms used as substitutes.

---

## Replacement Rules
The following common terms are explicitly replaced (frame translation):

- “Time management” → Time Sovereignty
- “Motivation / discipline” → Future Self Operating System
- “ICP” → Audience Identity
- “Niche” → Audience Identity or Category (context dependent)
- “Differentiation” → Resonance or Uncopyable Positioning
- “Product-market fit” → Resonance + Validation Signal
- “Personal brand” → Business (Vehicle)
- “Balance” → Full Stack Human
- “Hustle / grind” → Acting under uncertainty

---

## Term Introduction Rules
- New terms are introduced only after the problem they solve is felt.
- Framework names are not dropped without context.
- The first mention of a framework must include:
  - the problem it solves
  - what it replaces
- Never stack multiple new terms at once.

---

## Metaphor Rules
Approved metaphor families:
- Navigation (maps, direction, vehicles, beacons)
- Systems (operating systems, defaults, inputs/outputs)
- Identity (roles, authorship, future self)
- Concentration (compression, signal, resonance)

Avoid:
- war metaphors
- sports metaphors
- hustle metaphors
- overused startup clichés

---

## Category Design Language Rules
- Language must be immediately understandable.
- Language must anchor to a familiar problem or transformation.
- Differentiation should create curiosity, not confusion.
- Obscure or clever language is disallowed.
- Category names must be repeatable by others.

The goal is recognizability with intrigue.

---

## Forbidden Language Patterns
- Mixing corporate jargon with psychological language
- Using tactics to explain worldview problems
- Introducing frameworks out of sequence
- Using “success” without defining the dimension
- Talking about scale before resonance

---

## Break Language Rules

### Break (Pillar)
Break refers to the first pillar of transformation.
Break is structural and sustained, not momentary.

Rule: Break (the Pillar) is never used to describe a single insight or realization.

### Breaking Patterns / Breaking Limits
These phrases describe ongoing psychological and behavioral disruption.
Rule: This language implies repetition and effort, not instant change.

### Break Moment
A Break Moment is a specific moment of clarity, perspective shift, or insight.
Rule: A Break Moment is never treated as completion of the Break pillar (insight ≠ integration).

### Breakthrough (use sparingly)
Breakthrough refers to a noticeable shift that results from sustained breaking over time.
Rule: Use retrospectively, not as hype.

### Enforcement
- Do not collapse Break Moments into the Break pillar.
- Do not describe insight as transformation.
- Always distinguish between momentary clarity and sustained change.
