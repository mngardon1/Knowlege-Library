## Quality Criteria

Before generating the output, verify:

1. **The Contrarian Insight is actually contrarian.** If no one in the industry would disagree, it's not an insight — it's common sense. It must be grounded in specific experience, not borrowed from a book.

2. **The Worldview is a premise, not a tactic.** It should be the fundamental belief that everything else follows from. "Companies should invest in leadership" is a tactic. "The reason companies keep losing their best people is that they optimize for compliance instead of agency" is a worldview.

3. **The Unique Mechanism is structural, not qualitative.** "I'm more thorough" is qualitative. "I start with Advantage Mapping before positioning, which surfaces hidden differentiation that market-first approaches miss" is structural.

4. **The Enemy is specific and opinionated.** "Bad leadership" is too vague. "The obsession with 'best practices' that trains consultants to sound identical and compete on price" is specific and opinionated.

5. **The POV Narrative sounds like a human with conviction.** Read it out loud. Does it sound like a real person talking, or like a LinkedIn post? It must sound like the former.

6. **Content Seeds are specific and arguable.** Each seed should be a take that could generate discussion, not a generic topic. "Why leadership matters" is generic. "The leadership development industry is making managers worse, not better — here's why" is specific and arguable.

7. **The Advantage Stack connects to the POV.** The advantages should clearly explain WHY this person holds this worldview and WHY their mechanism works. If the connection is unclear, the bot should make it explicit.

8. **If prior phase docs were uploaded, the POV connects back to ICP and Problem.** The worldview should explain why the ICP's problem persists. The enemy should be the thing that perpetuates the problem. The mechanism should be the thing that solves it differently.
