# Phase 4: Synthesis

You've built the three pillars. Now we find out if they hold together.

Synthesis is where Person, Problem, and POV get assembled into a single, testable positioning statement. But this isn't just an assembly step. It's a quality check. The bot reads everything you've produced, finds where the pieces connect cleanly, and — more importantly — finds where they don't.

Inconsistencies between phases are normal. You may have defined your ICP in Phase 2 and then developed a POV in Phase 3 that points at a slightly different buyer. Or your problem statement uses different language than your ICP's pain bank. That's not failure — it's the work. Synthesis is where you resolve it.

Some inconsistencies can be resolved right now. Others need market feedback — real conversations with real buyers to find out what's actually true. The bot will help you distinguish between the two and build a validation plan for what you can't resolve in the chair.

---

## What to Expect

The Synthesis bot works in five steps:

1. **Consistency Audit** — reads all your documents and maps the connections. What aligns, what doesn't.
2. **Resolve or Flag** — for each mismatch, you'll either make a decision now or flag it for market validation.
3. **Draft** — the bot assembles three versions of your positioning statement: a full version (2-4 sentences), an elevator version (one sentence, under 20 words), and a narrative version (a paragraph for bios, about pages, and talks).
4. **Pressure Test** — five specific tests to validate the statement: Repeat-Back, Repulsion, Alternative, Plain Language, and Coffee Sentence.
5. **Validation Plan** — for anything that needs market feedback, the bot builds structured tests with specific actions, timelines, and signals to look for.

The output is your **Positioning Brief** — the master reference document for your business. It contains everything in one place: ICP summary, core problem, POV summary, all three positioning statement versions, audit results, test results, and your validation plan.

This is labeled **v1.0** — a hypothesis. Not a final answer. You'll test it in the market and refine.
