# Phase 3: POV

You know who you serve and what problem you solve. Now we answer the hardest question: why you?

There are other people who serve your ICP. Other people who address your problem. What makes you the one they should hire? It's not your resume. It's not your years of experience. It's how you see the world differently — and why that difference matters to your buyer.

Your POV is the intersection of four things: your unique advantages, a contrarian insight the market hasn't caught up to, a worldview about how your domain actually works, and a mechanism that explains why your approach works when conventional approaches don't.

This is also where you name your enemy — not a person, but a practice, a belief, or a way of doing things that keeps your ICP stuck. Having an enemy gives you something to stand against. That's what turns positioning into a platform.

---

## What to Expect

The POV bot works in two parts:

**Part 1: Advantages.** If you've already completed Advantage Mapping and have an Advantage Map, upload it and the bot will skip this section. If not, the bot will run a compressed extraction across five areas — Curiosity, Energy, Aptitudes, Expertise, and Decisions — to surface what makes you different. This is faster than the full Advantage Mapping process but covers what's needed for positioning.

**Part 2: POV Development.** The bot will push you to articulate four things:

1. **Contrarian Insight** — what you know from experience that contradicts conventional wisdom in your field
2. **Worldview** — the fundamental truth about your domain that most people miss
3. **Unique Mechanism** — how your approach works differently, not just better
4. **The Enemy** — the practice or belief you stand against

At the end, the bot will draft a POV Narrative — a first-person paragraph that weaves all four together. You'll read it out loud and refine it until it sounds like something you'd actually say.

The bot will reject safe answers. If your contrarian insight is something nobody would disagree with, it's not contrarian. If your worldview sounds like a LinkedIn post, it's not a worldview. Expect to be pushed.

The more the bot knows about your ICP and problem, the better it can connect your POV back to what your buyer actually cares about.
