# Phase 2: Person

You know roughly who you work with. The problem is "roughly" doesn't position you.

"I work with leaders at mid-size companies" could describe ten thousand people. Your positioning needs to describe one. Not a demographic — a situation. A specific person, at a specific moment, dealing with a specific version of the problem you just defined.

This phase locks in your Ideal Client Profile through three lenses: who they are on paper, how they think and make decisions, and the exact language they use to describe their own pain. That last part is the most important — it becomes the language you use in everything you write and say.

You'll also define who you're NOT for. This is where most consultants resist. It's also where the positioning gets real.

---

## What to Expect

The Person bot will take you through three lenses, one at a time:

1. **Demographic** — role, industry, company stage, buying power, trigger events. The facts.
2. **Psychographic** — beliefs, fears, decision-making style, identity, what they've already tried. How they think.
3. **Pain / Symptom Language** — the exact words they use to describe their problem. Not your diagnosis. Their words.

After all three lenses, the bot will push you to define explicit exclusions — who you will not work with, even if they have budget and interest.

If multiple ICPs emerge (they probably will), the bot will help you compare them and choose one primary. You can expand later. Right now, you commit to one.
