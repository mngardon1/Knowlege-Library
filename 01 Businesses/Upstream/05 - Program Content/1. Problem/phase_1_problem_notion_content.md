# Phase 1: Problem

You see a lot of problems in your industry. That's not the issue. The issue is you haven't committed to owning one.

Most consultants describe what they do instead of what their buyer struggles with. "I help companies with leadership development" is a solution looking for a problem. It doesn't make anyone lean in. It doesn't make anyone say "I need to talk to you."

This phase forces a choice. One problem. Stated in language your buyer actually uses. Specific enough to exclude people. Sharp enough to make the right person feel understood in five seconds.

---

## What to Expect

The Problem bot will walk you through four stages:

1. **Get everything on the table.** You'll dump every problem you see in your space. Don't filter yet.
2. **Buyer language test.** For each problem, the bot will push you to state it in your buyer's words — not yours.
3. **Evaluate and choose.** You'll assess each problem against evidence, urgency, specificity, and fit. Then pick one.
4. **Sharpen.** You'll refine the statement until it passes the dinner party test — if you said it to your ideal client, would they lean in or nod politely?

The bot will reject vague problem statements, solutions disguised as problems, and lists instead of choices. That's by design.
