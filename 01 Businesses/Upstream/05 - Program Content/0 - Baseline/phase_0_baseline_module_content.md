## Why We Start Here

Most consultants jump straight to tactics. More posts. More outreach. More coffee chats. But activity without clarity is just noise. You end up busy and still broke.

We don't do that here. We start upstream — at the root.

Your positioning sits on three pillars:

**Person** — Who specifically are you for? Not "leaders" or "companies." One person, in one situation, with one urgent need.

**Problem** — What is THE problem you solve? Not a list of capabilities. One problem, stated in language your buyer actually uses.

**POV** — What makes you different? Not your resume. Your perspective — what you believe, what you stand against, and why your approach works when others don't.

The baseline assessment tells us which of these three pillars is strong, which is developing, and which is missing entirely. That determines where we focus first.

---

## What to Expect

The assessment is a conversation with the Baseline Assessment bot. It takes about 10-15 minutes.

The bot will ask you questions across all three pillars — who you serve, what you solve, and what makes you different. It goes one question at a time. Answer honestly. Don't try to sound polished. The messier your answers, the more useful the assessment.

A few things about the experience:

**The bot will push you.** If your answers are vague, it will ask you to get specific. If you describe yourself in a way that could apply to a thousand other consultants, it will tell you. This isn't meant to be uncomfortable — it's meant to be honest. The scorecard is only useful if it reflects where you actually are, not where you want to be.

**There are no wrong answers. There are only vague ones.** Be specific. Use real client examples. Name real situations you've been in.

**It will ask you to confirm before generating the scorecard.** Near the end, the bot will reflect back a summary of what it heard across all three pillars. You'll have a chance to correct or add anything. Then it generates the scorecard.

**The output is your Positioning Scorecard** — a snapshot of where you stand right now, with each pillar rated as Strong, Developing, or Undeveloped. Your biggest gaps will be called out, along with a recommended starting point for Phase 1.

## Come Prepared

You don't need to do homework before this. But having a few things in mind will make the conversation sharper and faster.

**Think about your 2-3 best clients.** Not your biggest — your best. The ones where the work felt right, the results were strong, and you'd happily work with someone like them again. Be ready to describe what was happening in their world when they hired you.

**Think about a bad fit.** A client you took on and regretted. What was wrong? What made it a mismatch? This is just as useful as the good examples.

**Have your current language ready.** However you currently describe what you do — your LinkedIn headline, your elevator pitch, whatever you say when someone asks at a dinner party. The bot will ask for it. Don't rehearse a better version. Give it the real one.

**If you have any of the following, have them handy** (not required — just helpful if you have them):

- Your current website or LinkedIn profile
- An Advantage Map or Advantage Stack from a prior Advantage Mapping process (if you've gone through it)
- Any prior positioning work, brand messaging docs, or offer documents you've created
- Notes from recent sales conversations or client feedback

You can reference any of these during the conversation. The more specific and real your inputs, the more accurate the scorecard.

## After the Assessment: Save Your Scorecard

When the bot generates your Positioning Scorecard, it will appear as a block of formatted text.

**Follow these steps to save it:**

1. Copy the entire scorecard output (everything inside the code block)
2. Open a text editor (Notes, TextEdit, VS Code, Notion — anything that lets you save a file)
3. Paste the scorecard content
4. Save the file as `positioning-scorecard.md`
5. Move the file into your **`0 - Baseline`** folder in your document architecture

**This file is the starting point for the entire program.** You will upload it at the beginning of every subsequent phase. Keep it accessible.

Bring it to our next session. We'll review it together, discuss what stands out, and decide where to start Phase 1.
