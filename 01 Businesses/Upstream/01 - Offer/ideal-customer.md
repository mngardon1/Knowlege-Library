# Ideal Customer Profile — The Upstream Consultant
**Status:** REVIEW — lock after Michael confirms
**Governs:** All copy, content, outreach, and delivery asset language

---

## Who They Are

Independent consultant, 0–3 years out of corporate — or about to be. Mid-career, not early. Has real expertise — 10–20 years building something in a function, industry, or discipline. Left (or is leaving) a senior IC or management role at a company they didn't own.

Two starting points, same destination:

**Type 1 — Already started, stuck on pipeline**
Has clients or had them. Can't predict where the next one comes from. Relying on referrals and relationships that are starting to thin.

**Type 2 — Committed but not yet started**
Past the hedging phase. Done deciding. Has not yet landed a client but is fully committed to building — not dipping a toe in, not keeping a job as a fallback. Needs structure to do this right from the beginning.

Both types share the same want: to build something real, not just replace a salary.

**Demographics:**
- Age: 38–52
- Background: Corporate — finance, strategy, operations, HR, marketing, tech, consulting
- Income history: $150k–$400k+ in corporate
- Current consulting revenue: $0–$200k, inconsistent (or pre-revenue but committed)
- Location: US-based, remote-capable

---

## The Situation They're In

**If already started:** Has clients — or had them — but can't predict where the next one comes from. Every engagement feels like starting over. Outreach is random. LinkedIn posting is inconsistent. Referrals are thinning. The feast-or-famine cycle is real — some months fine, others quiet enough to check job boards.

**If not yet started:** Has made the decision. Not hedging, not testing — committed. But has no structure for how to actually build this. Knows hustle alone won't work. Wants to do it right from day one rather than spend two years figuring out what the system should have been.

In both cases: they know their work is good. They don't know how to make that legible to the market consistently. And they want more than income — they want to build something that grows.

---

## What They Want

- To build a real business — not a job replacement, not a lifestyle hedge
- A full pipeline. Options. To be choosing clients, not chasing them.
- Confidence that the business works even when they're not grinding.
- To charge what they're worth without justifying it every time.
- To create something they own that compounds over time — audience, IP, reputation

---

## What They Won't Say Out Loud

*These are the truths they carry but rarely admit:*

- "I'm still looking at jobs just in case."
- "Every new client feels like luck."
- "I don't know if I can actually make this work long-term."
- "I'm undercharging and I know it but I don't know how to stop."
- "I'm busy but I'm not building anything."
- "I left corporate to have more control. I have less."

---

## What's Actually Blocking Them

Not effort. Not capability. Not mindset.

**Infrastructure.** They don't have:
- A sharp, externally-legible positioning statement
- A system for generating pipeline independent of their energy
- A conversion process that doesn't require them to perform on every call
- An AI layer that keeps content and outreach moving without consuming their time

They're trying to solve a systems problem with hustle. It doesn't work.

---

## Psychology

- High competence, low confidence in the market-facing side of the business
- Identity partially still attached to their corporate title or company
- Loss aversion is strong — checking job boards is the relief valve
- Intellectually knows what they need to do; emotionally resistant to committing
- Responds to diagnosis, not motivation — they want to be seen clearly, not cheered on
- Trusts people who've done it, not people who've studied it

---

## What Moves Them

- Someone who names exactly what they're living without them having to explain it
- Evidence that the problem is structural, not personal — they're not failing, they're missing infrastructure
- A clear, sequenced path — not a framework, a system with an order of operations
- Proof that AI can actually do this (they're skeptical but curious)
- A peer group at the same stage — they don't want to be the only one

---

## What Doesn't Move Them

- Generic "mindset" or "believe in yourself" language
- Vague promises about "transformation"
- Urgency and pressure tactics
- Programs that feel like courses — they want a system, not content
- Anyone who hasn't actually consulted

---

## Exact Language (Use This, Not Paraphrases)

> "I was still looking at jobs a year after I decided I wasn't."
> "Every client feels like luck."
> "I don't have a sense of what I should do or where I bring the most value."
> "Nobody gave me objectives. I finally control my destiny. I'm crippled."
> "I'm busy but I'm not building anything."
> "I left to have more control. I have less."

---

## Disqualifiers

- Needs revenue in the next 30 days — not ready
- Still treating consulting as a bridge to another job
- Unwilling to be challenged or do the work
- Believes AI is a gimmick

---

## The Core Tension

They left corporate for autonomy. They've recreated dependency — on referrals, relationships, and luck. The Upstream program restores the agency they left to find.
