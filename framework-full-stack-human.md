# Framework — Full Stack Human

## Definition
A Full Stack Human is an individual who intentionally develops and integrates
the core dimensions of human life rather than optimizing a single domain in
isolation.

Instead of defining success through one role or outcome, the Full Stack Human
orients their life around a coherent system that supports long-term vitality,
meaning, and agency.

This framework treats human wholeness as a design problem, not a personality trait.

---

## Problem It Solves
The Full Stack Human framework explains why:
- career success often coincides with personal emptiness
- financial achievement can come at the cost of health or relationships
- high performers feel fragmented or misaligned
- people delay living fully until “later”

Without this framework, individuals default to single-stack optimization —
typically career or income — and attempt to patch the consequences reactively.

---

## What It Replaces
The Full Stack Human framework replaces the belief that:
- success in one area can compensate for collapse in others
- life should be optimized sequentially (career first, life later)
- sacrifice in health, relationships, or meaning is temporary
- wholeness is a luxury rather than a requirement
