# Framework — Advantage Mapping

## Definition
Advantage Mapping is the process of identifying and articulating the unique
combination of experiences, perspectives, skills, and pattern recognition that
allows an individual to solve specific problems more effectively than others.

Rather than relying on credentials or comparison, Advantage Mapping treats value
as emergent from lived experience and systems thinking.

An advantage is defined by usefulness in context, not rarity in isolation.

---

## Problem It Solves
Advantage Mapping explains why people:
- undervalue what comes naturally to them
- believe others are more qualified
- struggle to articulate their value
- copy existing models instead of building from themselves
- chase ideas that aren’t aligned with their strengths

Without this framework, individuals default to external validation systems
(resumes, titles, niches) that obscure their real leverage.

---

## What It Replaces
Advantage Mapping replaces the belief that:
- value comes from credentials alone
- expertise must be narrow to be useful
- what feels easy is not valuable
- legitimacy must be granted externally
- confidence should come before clarity
