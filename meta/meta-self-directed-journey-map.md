# Meta Model — The Self-Directed Journey Map

## Purpose
The Self-Directed Journey Map is an orientation model used to help individuals
understand where they are in their journey and what they need next, without
confusing movement with progress.

It is not a framework and does not explain how the world works.
It exists to reduce disorientation during transition.

---

## The Four Elements of Any Self-Directed Journey

### 1. Direction — Future Vision
A sense of where you want to go.
This is not a goal, but a directional pull that informs decisions.

Without direction, effort becomes scattered.

---

### 2. Position — Advantage Map
An honest understanding of where you currently are.
This includes strengths, lived experience, perspective, and leverage.

Without position, people default to comparison or credentials.

---

### 3. Vehicle — The Business
The mechanism that moves you from current reality toward your future.
The business is a vehicle, not an identity.

Without a vehicle, insight never compounds.

---

### 4. Navigation — Beacons
Recognizable markers that confirm you are on the right path.
These include Desire Maps, milestones, and felt-state indicators.

Without navigation, uncertainty is mistaken for failure.

---

## How This Model Is Used
- To orient people at the beginning of the journey
- To normalize uncertainty during transitions
- To identify which element is missing when someone feels stuck
- To explain why certain work matters at different times

This model supports Break → Become → Build without replacing it.
