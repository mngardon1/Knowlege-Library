# Framework — Category Design

## Definition
Category Design is the practice of articulating a distinct category using clear,
common, and understandable language that carries just enough differentiation and
curiosity to be memorable.

It combines Audience Identity and Uncopyable Positioning into a recognizable
mental association so that when someone thinks of a specific problem or desired
transformation, they think of a specific business or process.

Category Design does not create resonance — it names it.

---

## Problem It Solves
Category Design explains why people:
- struggle to explain what they do succinctly
- blend into existing categories despite strong results
- rely on long explanations instead of instant recognition
- lose referrals due to unclear positioning
- feel obvious to some but invisible to others

Without Category Design, resonance remains implicit and difficult to transmit.

---

## What It Replaces
Category Design replaces the belief that:
- differentiation requires obscure or clever language
- clarity and memorability are opposites
- branding is primarily visual
- categories must already exist to be valid
- naming comes before proof

---

## Language Constraints (Non-Negotiable)
Effective Category Design language must:
- be immediately understandable without explanation
- anchor to familiar concepts or problems
- include a differentiated edge that creates curiosity
- feel specific rather than generic
- be repeatable by others

Language that is too abstract loses clarity.
Language that is too generic loses distinction.

The goal is recognizability with intrigue.

---

## Downstream Role of Category Design
Category Design sits upstream of all marketing execution.

Once a category is clearly articulated, it informs:
- messaging
- content themes
- advertising
- landing pages
- sales conversations

Marketing does not discover the category.
Marketing expresses the category.

Without Category Design, marketing becomes inconsistent, effortful, and noisy.
