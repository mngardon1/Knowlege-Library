## The Central Belief (Non-Negotiable)

**Humans are more flexible, adaptable, and capable than they believe — and most people never find out because they never test it.**

The structural drift of capitalism (dynamic → static) is an explainer, not an excuse. It describes how the conditions changed. It does not justify passivity. The individual always had more agency than they used. The problem is not the system — it's the acceptance of the system's definition of what's possible.

Michael's worldview in one sentence: *You are responsible for where you are, and you are responsible for where you go — and that is the most liberating truth available to you.*

The structural critique exists to name the problem clearly, not to assign blame. Once you see the drift, the only move worth making is action.

## Core Intellectual Anchors

- **Adam Smith / Locke** — The original dynamic promise of capitalism: distributed agency, symmetric risk, value connection. What we've drifted from.
- **Marcus Aurelius / Stoicism** — Locus of control. What you own vs. what owns you. The internal citadel.
- **Robert Pirsig (Zen and the Art of Motorcycle Maintenance)** — Dynamic vs. Static Quality. The tension between preserving what works and the creative force that advances things. Michael's Dynamic/Static capitalism framework is directly downstream of this.
- **Richard Boyatzis (Intentional Change Theory)** — Humans develop through self-directed vision, not imposed pathways. Coaching must start from the ideal self, not the gap.
- **Kahneman / Prospect Theory** — Loss aversion is the psychological trap keeping people stuck. Understanding variance (not just downside risk) unlocks agency.

## Worldview: Dynamic vs. Static

The core tension Michael returns to across all his work:

| Dynamic | Static |
|---|---|
| Self-directed goals | Company-selected path |
| Symmetric risk | Risk-blind dependency |
| Value connection | Abstracted contribution |
| Agency | Compliance |
| Optionality | Optimization |
| Builder | Maintainer |

The Static promise masquerades as security. It is actually fragility — because it suppresses the adaptive capacity humans need to navigate real-world disruption.

The Dynamic promise is not chaos. It is the restoration of individual ownership over time, value, risk, and options.

## The Four Levers of Reclamation

Michael's individual-level framework from The Reclamation (Part 3 of the Substack series):

1. **Time** — Own it. Dynamic Time Value: time has directed value, not fixed value. Reclaim up to 40% by protecting high-leverage hours.
2. **Value** — Your resume is a translation, not the original. Identify portable, underlying capability — not role-specific artifacts.
3. **Risk** — Most people manage the wrong risk (loyalty). Real risk management = seeing variance clearly (both sides) and building against exogenous shocks, not internal favor.
4. **Optionality** — Not a Plan B. A practice of keeping real pathways in motion. Reduces downside AND expands upside simultaneously.

## Coaching Philosophy

- Change starts with self-direction, not imposed gap analysis
- People don't lack capability — they lack a clear view of their own value outside the container they've been in
- The goal is not to help someone get a better job — it's to restore agency so they can define what "better" means themselves
- Coaching is not validation. It is the challenge to see more clearly.
- Identity-performance fusion is the root blocker: people confuse who they are with what they do, which makes any change feel like a threat to existence
- The system Boyatzis describes (Intentional Change Theory) maps directly: ideal self → real self → learning agenda → experimentation → trusting relationships
