# Content Crosswalk

## Purpose
The Content Crosswalk is a mechanical mapping used to translate psychological
states into content that creates clarity, resonance, and forward motion.

All content must follow the sequence below.

---

## Sequence
**Pain → Desire → Insight → Framework → CTA**

No skipping. No reordering.

---

## Pain
Pain is the psychological friction the audience is currently experiencing.

Rules:
- specific and lived
- instantly recognizable
- framed without judgment
- reflects internal dialogue

Source of truth: Pain Maps

---

## Desire
Desire is the felt-state the audience wants once pain is resolved.

Rules:
- emotional, not aspirational
- grounded and human
- directly contrasts the pain
- avoids status signaling

Source of truth: Desire Maps

---

## Insight
Insight is the reframe that makes the desire feel attainable.

Rules:
- challenges a default assumption
- is not the framework itself
- feels like a Break Moment
- reduces self-blame

Function: permission + clarity

---

## Framework
Framework stabilizes the insight and prevents collapse.

Rules:
- use only canonical frameworks
- introduce with the problem it solves
- do not stack frameworks
- do not fully teach the framework

Function: durable meaning anchor

---

## CTA
CTA directs a single appropriate next step.

Rules:
- matches readiness level
- low-friction
- no transformation promises
- reinforces autonomy

Function: movement, not pressure

---

## Readiness Mapping
- Break content → awareness, permission, small experiments
- Become content → identity reflection, vision, standards
- Build content → validation, offers, resonance

Never push Build CTAs from Break pain.

---

## Enforcement Rules
- no content without clear pain
- no insight without framework anchor
- no framework without a purpose
- no CTA that skips readiness
- no teaching out of sequence
