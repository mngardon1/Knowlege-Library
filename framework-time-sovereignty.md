# Framework — Time Sovereignty

## Definition
Time Sovereignty is the condition in which an individual experiences full
psychological ownership over their time and consciously allocates it according
to self-directed priorities rather than default demands.

It is not about working less or managing time better.
It is about reclaiming authorship over how time is valued, defended, and invested.

Time Sovereignty marks the transition from reactive living to intentional living.

---

## Problem It Solves
Time Sovereignty explains why people:
- feel constantly busy yet disconnected
- optimize productivity without feeling progress
- believe they “don’t have time” for what matters
- experience guilt when prioritizing themselves
- confuse availability with value

Without Time Sovereignty, time is unconsciously treated as something:
- owned by employers
- claimed by others
- sold by the hour
- consumed by urgency rather than intention

---

## What It Replaces
Time Sovereignty replaces the belief that:
- time management is the solution to misalignment
- being busy means being valuable
- availability equals commitment
- time must be earned before it can be claimed
- external demands should determine internal priorities
