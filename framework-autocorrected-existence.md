# Framework — Autocorrected Existence

## Definition
Autocorrected Existence is the condition in which a person’s life direction is
silently substituted by default societal systems rather than consciously chosen.

In this state, individuals begin with vague intentions or early dreams, but over
time their direction is “corrected” toward culturally approved paths that
prioritize stability, status, income, and external validation.

The substitution is subtle, well-intentioned, and reinforced by institutions,
norms, and incentives — making it largely invisible to the person living inside it.

---

## Problem It Solves
Autocorrected Existence explains why capable, intelligent people:
- work hard without feeling fulfilled
- optimize for promotions instead of alignment
- pursue goals that feel increasingly hollow
- feel stuck without knowing why
- struggle to imagine alternatives that feel legitimate

Without naming Autocorrected Existence, people assume the problem is:
- lack of discipline
- insufficient gratitude
- fear
- or personal inadequacy

In reality, they are responding rationally to a default system that quietly
overwrites authorship.

---

## What It Replaces
Autocorrected Existence replaces the belief that:
- “This is just how life works”
- “Everyone feels this way”
- “I should be grateful and stop wanting more”
- “The safest path is the right path”
- “Success naturally leads to meaning”
